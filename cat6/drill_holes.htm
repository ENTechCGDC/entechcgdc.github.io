5. Drill Holes

 
  • If drilling through subfloor:
  • Inspect the bottom of the stud bay for debris and other obstacles. Remove any debris that you can, and avoid obstacles when you begin drilling.
  • Install an appropriately sized flex bit into your drill and guide the bit into the wall, bringing it to rest on the bottom plate. Begin drilling until you break through the plate and subfloor into the crawlspace or basement.
 
  • If drilling through top plate in attic:
  • Locate the correct portion of wall’s top plate in attic.
  • Install an appropriately sized installer’s or bell-hanger bit into your drill and drill through the top plate and cap into the stud bay
 
  • Using an appropriate drill bit, drill remaining holes through framing members that cannot be passed over / under. This is often not necessary in an attic but may be desirable in an unfinished basement where all wiring is run through the floor joists.
     
    
    If you drill holes in a floor joist, only drill in the middle 1/3 to avoid damaging the integrity of the framing.
